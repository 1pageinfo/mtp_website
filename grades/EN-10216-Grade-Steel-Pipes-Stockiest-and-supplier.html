Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What are EN 10216 Grade Steel Pipes?

EN 10216 Grade Steel Pipes are seamless steel tubes manufactured according to European standards for pressure purposes. They are designed to operate under high pressure and elevated temperature conditions, making them suitable for boilers, heat exchangers, and pressure vessel applications.

What is the difference between EN 10216-1 and EN 10216-2?

EN 10216-1 covers non-alloy steel tubes for pressure purposes at room temperature, while EN 10216-2 specifies non-alloy and alloy steel tubes with specified elevated temperature properties. EN 10216-2 grades are commonly used in high-temperature services.

Which grades are commonly supplied under EN 10216?

Common EN 10216 grades include P195TR1, P235TR2, P265TR2 for non-alloy steel applications, and P265GH, P355GH, and 16Mo3 for elevated temperature and pressure service.

Are EN 10216 steel pipes seamless or welded?

EN 10216 Grade Steel Pipes are supplied in seamless form. Seamless construction provides higher strength, uniform structure, and improved resistance to pressure compared to welded pipes.
