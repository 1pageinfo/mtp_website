Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What are Incoloy 600 Pipes?

Incoloy 600 Pipes are made from a nickel-chromium alloy designed for high-temperature and corrosive environments. They offer excellent resistance to oxidation, carburization, and chloride-ion stress corrosion cracking.

What is the UNS number for Incoloy 600?

The UNS designation for Incoloy 600 is UNS N06600. This number is commonly used in international specifications and material certifications.

Which standards cover Incoloy 600 Pipes?

Incoloy 600 Pipes are primarily manufactured according to ASTM B167 and ASME SB167 standards. They may also comply with other international norms depending on project requirements.

Are Incoloy 600 Pipes available in seamless and welded forms?

Yes, Incoloy 600 Pipes are available in both seamless and welded forms. Seamless pipes are preferred for high-pressure applications, while welded pipes are used for general industrial purposes.

What are the main applications of Incoloy 600 Pipes?

These pipes are widely used in chemical processing, heat exchangers, nuclear power plants, furnaces, petrochemical units, and power generation industries.

Do Incoloy 600 Pipes resist high temperatures?

Yes, Incoloy 600 Pipes perform exceptionally well at elevated temperatures and maintain strength and stability up to approximately 1093°C.

Are Incoloy 600 Pipes resistant to corrosion?

Incoloy 600 Pipes offer excellent resistance to oxidation, alkaline corrosion, and many organic and inorganic acids, making them suitable for aggressive environments.

Can Incoloy 600 Pipes be welded easily?

Yes, Incoloy 600 Pipes have good weldability and can be welded using standard welding techniques. Post-weld heat treatment is generally not required.

What sizes are available for Incoloy 600 Pipes?

Incoloy 600 Pipes are available in a wide range of diameters, wall thicknesses, schedules, and custom lengths to meet specific project needs.

What tests are performed on Incoloy 600 Pipes?

Common tests include hydrostatic testing, ultrasonic testing, eddy current testing, PMI, and visual inspection to ensure quality and compliance.
