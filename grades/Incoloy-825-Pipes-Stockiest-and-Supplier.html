Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What is Incoloy 825?

Incoloy 825 is a nickel-iron-chromium alloy with added molybdenum, copper, and titanium. It is designed to provide exceptional resistance to both reducing and oxidizing acids, making it suitable for highly corrosive environments.

2. What are Incoloy 825 pipes used for?

Incoloy 825 pipes are widely used in chemical processing, oil and gas, pollution control equipment, acid production, pickling operations, nuclear fuel reprocessing, and marine applications.

3. What standards are Incoloy 825 pipes manufactured to?

These pipes are commonly manufactured according to ASTM B423, ASTM B705, and ASME SB423 standards, depending on the type (seamless or welded) and application requirements.

4. What makes Incoloy 825 corrosion resistant?

The high nickel content provides resistance to chloride-ion stress corrosion cracking, while chromium protects against oxidizing environments. Molybdenum and copper enhance resistance to pitting, crevice corrosion, and sulfuric acid.

5. Are Incoloy 825 pipes suitable for high-temperature service?

Yes, Incoloy 825 pipes retain good mechanical strength and corrosion resistance at elevated temperatures, making them suitable for moderately high-temperature applications.

6. What forms of Incoloy 825 pipes are available?

Incoloy 825 pipes are available in seamless, welded, and ERW forms, with options for round, square, and rectangular shapes as per customer requirements.

7. Can Incoloy 825 pipes be welded easily?

Yes, Incoloy 825 has good weldability using conventional welding techniques. Matching filler metals are generally recommended to maintain corrosion resistance and mechanical properties.

8. What heat treatment is required for Incoloy 825 pipes?

Incoloy 825 pipes are usually supplied in the annealed condition to achieve optimal corrosion resistance and mechanical performance.

9. How do Incoloy 825 pipes perform in acidic environments?

These pipes show excellent resistance to sulfuric, phosphoric, and nitric acids, as well as mixed acid environments, which makes them ideal for chemical and fertilizer industries.

10. Are Incoloy 825 pipes available in custom sizes?

Yes, manufacturers and suppliers offer Incoloy 825 pipes in a wide range of standard and custom sizes, wall thicknesses, and lengths to meet specific project requirements.
