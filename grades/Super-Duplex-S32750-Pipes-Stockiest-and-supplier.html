Super Duplex S32750 Pipes Stockiest and Supplier

Super Duplex S32750 Pipes are high-performance stainless steel pipes made from a super duplex stainless grade (UNS S32750 / 2507) that combines the strength of ferritic and austenitic microstructures. This special alloy boasts elevated levels of chromium (~24/26%), nickel (~6/8%), molybdenum (~3/5%), and nitrogen (~0.24/0.32%), which results in exceptional corrosion resistance and mechanical strength in aggressive environments. These pipes significantly outperform traditional austenitic grades (such as 316L) in resistance to pitting, crevice corrosion and chloride-induced stress corrosion cracking, making them ideal for extremely demanding industrial uses.

The mechanical performance of Super Duplex S32750 Pipes is one of their key strengths. With tensile strengths often exceeding 800 MPa and yield strengths around 550 MPa, these pipes provide structural integrity under heavy loads and pressures. Their low thermal expansion and high thermal conductivity further support reliability in fluctuating thermal conditions. These characteristics allow designers to use thinner walls for equivalent strength, reducing weight and material costs in critical systems.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What are Super Duplex S32750 Pipes used for?

Super Duplex S32750 Pipes are primarily used in highly corrosive and high-pressure environments such as oil & gas production, offshore platforms, chemical processing, desalination plants, marine systems, and power generation industries. What does S32750 mean?

S32750 is the UNS designation for Super Duplex Stainless Steel 2507. It indicates a high-alloy duplex stainless steel with excellent resistance to pitting, crevice corrosion, and stress corrosion cracking, along with high mechanical strength.

Which standards are Super Duplex S32750 Pipes manufactured to?
