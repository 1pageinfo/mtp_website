Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What are ASTM A312 TP 904L Pipes & Tubes used for?

ASTM A312 TP 904L Pipes & Tubes are used in highly corrosive environments such as chemical processing, petrochemical plants, oil & gas refineries, marine applications, and heat exchangers. Their high nickel, molybdenum, and copper content provides excellent resistance to acids, chlorides, and pitting corrosion.

What does TP 904L mean in ASTM A312?

TP stands for Tube/Pipe, and 904L is a low-carbon, high-alloy austenitic stainless steel grade. It is designed for superior corrosion resistance, especially against sulfuric acid, phosphoric acid, and seawater environments.

Are ASTM A312 TP 904L Pipes & Tubes suitable for high-temperature applications?

Yes, ASTM A312 TP 904L Pipes & Tubes perform well at elevated temperatures and retain good mechanical strength. However, maximum allowable temperatures depend on pressure and design codes such as ASME B31.3.

What manufacturing forms are available for ASTM A312 TP 904L Pipes & Tubes?

ASTM A312 TP 904L Pipes & Tubes are available in Seamless (SMLS) and Welded forms (ERW / EFW). They are typically supplied in solution-annealed and pickled condition to ensure optimal corrosion resistance.

Is ASTM A312 TP 904L available in PSL 1 or PSL 2?

No. PSL 1 and PSL 2 classifications apply only to API 5L line pipes. ASTM A312 TP 904L Pipes & Tubes are supplied as per ASTM/ASME requirements and purchaser-specific testing criteria.
